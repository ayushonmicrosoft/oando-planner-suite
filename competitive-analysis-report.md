# Competitive Analysis: One&Only (oando.co.in)
## Office Planning SaaS — Competitive Landscape Report

**Date:** July 2025
**Product:** One&Only — Office Furniture & Workspace Planning SaaS
**URL:** oando.co.in
**Target Market:** India-first, expanding globally
**Target Customers:** Corporates, architects, interior designers, facility managers

---

## Executive Summary

### Positioning Statement (April Dunford Framework)

**For** corporates, architects, and facility managers in India **who** need to plan and visualize office furniture layouts digitally, **One&Only** is an office workspace planning SaaS **that** combines 2D canvas planning, guided blueprint wizards, professional-grade tldraw-powered live planning, 3D visualization, and CAD tools in a single web platform — with an India-focused furniture catalog. **Unlike** SmartDraw (general diagramming), Floorplanner (consumer-focused), or pCon.planner (desktop-only), **we** deliver an integrated browser-based suite purpose-built for Indian office furniture planning with real catalog items, AI layout advice, and multi-format export.

### Top 3 Strategic Recommendations

1. **Own the "India Office Planning" category** — No direct competitor offers an India-focused office furniture planning tool with local catalog items. Position aggressively as the go-to platform for Indian corporates and architects.
2. **Lead with the integrated suite story** — Competitors force users to stitch together multiple tools (2D planner + 3D viewer + CAD + export). One&Only's all-in-one platform eliminates tool-switching friction.
3. **Exploit the pricing gap** — Direct competitors charge $5–$30+/month for comparable features, many without India-specific content. Offer a compelling free tier + affordable pro plan to capture the Indian market before global players localize.

**Key Finding:** There is a significant gap in the Indian market. No dedicated, India-focused office planning SaaS tool exists with the following characteristics:

## 6. Market Gaps One&Only Can Exploit

### Gap 1: No India-Focused Office Planning SaaS Exists
**Evidence:** None of the 7 direct competitors offer India-specific office furniture catalogs, ISI-standard furniture dimensions, or templates for common Indian office configurations (IT parks, government offices, co-working spaces). Livspace and HomeLane are service companies, not SaaS tools.

**Opportunity:** Become the default office planning tool for the Indian market by building:
- India-specific furniture catalog with local manufacturer SKUs
- Templates for Indian office types (IT/ITES, government, banking, manufacturing)
- NBC (National Building Code) compliance guidelines in templates
- INR pricing and GST-compliant quotation generation

### Gap 2: No All-in-One Web Platform Combines 2D + 3D + CAD + AI
**Evidence:** Users currently need SmartDraw or Floorplanner for 2D + SketchUp or Coohom for 3D + AutoCAD for precision. No single web tool covers all three planning modalities plus AI assistance.

**Opportunity:** One&Only already has this integration. Market the "one tool to replace three" narrative aggressively. Quantify time saved vs. tool-switching workflows.

### Gap 3: Professional Drawing Tools at Consumer Pricing
**Evidence:** pCon.planner and 2020 Office offer professional features but are desktop-only and expensive ($1,500+/yr). Consumer tools (Planner 5D, Floorplanner) are cheap but lack CAD precision. There's a gap for web-based professional tools at SMB pricing.

**Opportunity:** Position One&Only's tldraw-powered Live Planner and CAD tools as "professional-grade, browser-based, SMB-priced."

### Gap 4: Guided Onboarding for Non-Designers
**Evidence:** None of the direct competitors offer a blueprint wizard or guided room setup flow. All assume users know how to start from a blank canvas.

**Opportunity:** The Blueprint Wizard is a unique differentiator. Market it specifically to facility managers and corporate administrators who are not designers but need to plan spaces.

### Gap 5: Template Library for Office-Specific Use Cases
**Evidence:** Most competitors' template libraries focus on residential layouts. Office-specific templates (open plan, cubicle layouts, conference rooms, reception areas) are sparse across the market.

**Opportunity:** Build the largest office-specific template library. Include Indian corporate standards, hot-desking layouts, hybrid work configurations, and industry-specific setups (banking halls, call centers, R&D labs).

## 7. Strategic Positioning Recommendations

### 7.1 Category Creation: "India's Office Planning Platform"
Don't compete in the generic "floor plan software" category where Floorplanner and SmartDraw have decades of headstart. Create and own a new category: **"Office Planning Platform for India."** This narrows the competitive field to zero current players.

**Action Items:**
- SEO: Target "office layout planner India," "office furniture planning tool," "office space design software India"
- Content: Publish case studies with Indian corporate clients (Tata Motors, Maruti Suzuki, L&T, JSW — already visible in client logos)
- PR: Position in Indian business/tech media (YourStory, Inc42, ET Tech)

### 7.2 Freemium Strategy to Capture Volume
**Recommendation:** Offer a generous free tier (up to 3 projects, full feature access, watermarked exports) to build a user base. Convert on team features, unlimited exports, and priority AI advice.

**Pricing Framework Suggestion:**
| Tier | Price | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Free | ₹0 | 3 projects, all tools, watermarked exports |
| Pro | ₹499–999/mo | Unlimited projects, no watermark, priority AI, SVG export |
| Team | ₹1,999–2,999/mo | Collaboration, shared templates, admin controls |
| Enterprise | Custom | SSO, API access, custom catalog, SLA support |

This undercuts SmartDraw ($9.95/mo ≈ ₹830/mo) and Floorplanner ($29/mo ≈ ₹2,400/mo for Pro) while offering more office-specific value.

### 7.3 Lean Into What Competitors Cannot Copy Quickly
**Unique advantages to amplify:**
1. **Blueprint Wizard** — No competitor has guided office setup. Perfect for non-designers.
2. **tldraw-based Live Planner** — Professional-grade collaborative drawing that's open-source powered and extensible.
3. **India-first catalog** — Building a localized furniture database takes years. First-mover advantage is defensible.
4. **React Three Fiber 3D viewer** — Modern, web-native 3D (not legacy WebGL/Flash).

### 7.4 Build the Indian Office Furniture Ecosystem
**Long-term moat strategy:**
- Partner with Indian furniture manufacturers (Godrej Interio, Featherlite, Durian, Wipro Furniture) to integrate real product catalogs
- Enable "plan → quote → order" workflow with manufacturer integration
- This replicates pCon.planner and 2020 Office's dealer model but for the Indian market — a $2B+ office furniture industry

### 7.5 Target Underserved Segments
| Segment | Current Solution | One&Only Pitch |
|---|---|---|
| **Indian IT/ITES Companies** | Hired architects + AutoCAD | Self-service planning for facility managers, 10x faster |
| **Co-working Space Operators** | Manual + Excel | Template-based layout optimization for multiple configurations |
| **Government Offices** | Manual drawing + PowerPoint | NBC-compliant templates, digital record-keeping |
| **Small Architects (1-5 person firms)** | AutoCAD (pirated) or SketchUp Free | Legal, affordable, purpose-built alternative |
| **Corporate Real Estate Teams** | 2020 Office ($5,000/yr) or consultants | Same capabilities at 1/10th the cost |

### 7.6 Competitive Battlecard: Key Selling Points

**When competing against SmartDraw:**
- "SmartDraw is a general diagramming tool that happens to do floor plans. One&Only is built exclusively for office planning."
- Trap-setting question: "Does SmartDraw have an office furniture catalog with real product dimensions? Can you view your plan in 3D?"

**When competing against Floorplanner:**
- "Floorplanner is great for residential. For office planning, you need office-specific templates, furniture catalogs, and CAD-grade tools — which Floorplanner doesn't offer."
- Trap-setting question: "Can Floorplanner generate a site plan? Does it have a guided wizard for non-designers?"

**When competing against AutoCAD:**
- "AutoCAD costs ₹16,000/month and requires training. One&Only gives your facility team professional office planning tools they can use in 5 minutes."
- Trap-setting question: "How long does it take to train a facility manager on AutoCAD? What's the cost per seat?"

**When competing against "do nothing" (Excel/PowerPoint):**
- "You're spending 4 hours arranging rectangles in PowerPoint. One&Only gives you a to-scale floor plan with real furniture in 15 minutes."
- Trap-setting question: "When you present a PowerPoint layout to leadership, can they visualize the space in 3D? Can you export dimensions for the furniture vendor?"
