## Executive Summary

The office planning and furniture SaaS market sits at the intersection of three powerful forces: the $57-68 billion global office furniture industry, the $5+ billion Integrated Workplace Management Systems (IWMS) market, and the rapidly expanding $1-2 billion space planning software segment. All three are growing at healthy CAGRs of 5-14%, driven by hybrid work adoption, smart office technologies, and the global reconfiguration of workspaces.

India represents one of the fastest-growing opportunities globally. With record-breaking office absorption of 79 million sq. ft. in 2024, a $5.8 billion office furniture market growing at 8-9% CAGR, and over 1,950 Global Capability Centers (GCCs) driving 36-40% of office leasing, the addressable market for office planning SaaS in India is estimated at $200-400 million in 2025, scaling to $600-900 million by 2030.

The technology landscape is rapidly evolving: AI-powered generative layout design, AR/VR visualization, 3D product configurators, and digital twins are transitioning from novelties to essential capabilities. Companies like Steelcase (with Casey AI), Foyr Neo, and qbiq.ai are pioneering AI-first approaches, while traditional players integrate 2D/3D planning tools via platforms like pCon.planner and Configura.

For One&Only, the convergence of India's commercial real estate boom, the Make in India furniture manufacturing push, and the technology gap in India-specific office planning tools creates a significant strategic opportunity.

## 2. Key Trends

### 2.1 Hybrid Work and Workspace Reconfiguration

Hybrid work has fundamentally transformed office design requirements:

- **80%** of HR leaders report hybrid work boosts productivity (Global Workplace Research)
- **90%** of CEOs use hybrid models to reduce overhead costs (KPMG 2025 Global CEO Outlook)
- **83%** of global CEOs expect full-time return-to-office by 2027, creating tension with employee preferences (KPMG)
- **42%** of hybrid workers report "thriving" versus lower rates for fully remote or fully in-office workers (Gallup, State of Global Workplace 2025)
- CBRE reports that the importance of density as a KPI has **plummeted 67%** over four years, while employee satisfaction has **surged 75%** in importance

**Impact on office planning:** Organizations are shifting from fixed desk assignments to flexible, bookable spaces. This drives demand for space planning software that can model different occupancy scenarios, manage hot-desking, and optimize space utilization in real-time.

### 2.2 Activity-Based Working (ABW)

Activity-Based Working -- where employees choose from a variety of settings based on the task at hand -- is gaining momentum:

- Employee satisfaction increases by **+17%** after switching to ABW (Veldhoen + Company)
- Gen Z spends **12% more time** in office compared to older cohorts, but demands different space types (Work Design Magazine)
- Biophilic design elements deliver **+15% productivity gains** and **-35% absenteeism reduction** (Global Workplace Research)

ABW requires diverse furniture solutions: focus pods, collaboration zones, phone booths, casual meeting areas, and traditional workstations -- all needing to be planned and visualized before procurement.

### 2.3 Smart Offices and IoT

The smart building market reached **$103 billion in 2024** and is projected to grow to $827.7 billion by 2034 at a 24.4% CAGR (GM Insights):

- AI-enabled software for occupancy analysis rose by **46% between 2022 and 2024**
- Smart building automation integration with space planning systems increased by **33%**
- Digital twin technology for buildings is a **$4.68 billion market in 2024**, projected to reach $32-63 billion by 2033 at 27-33% CAGR
- AI in office space analytics can cut operational costs by up to **25%** and reduce carbon emissions by up to **30%**

India has added more than **6,500 smart facilities** under the Smart Cities program with occupancy tracking and automated compliance features.

### 2.4 Sustainability

Sustainability is becoming a competitive differentiator and regulatory requirement:

- EU sustainability regulations came into force in December 2024, affecting furniture manufacturers globally
- Wood holds 45.2% of material market share, while metal is growing fastest at 7.6% CAGR
- Green building certifications (LEED, IGBC in India) increasingly require documented space efficiency and material sustainability
- Circular furniture models (like NORNORM in Europe) are gaining traction with subscription-based rental approaches
- Premium sustainable products command higher margins, with the premium segment growing at 8.6% CAGR vs. 5-7% for economy

|---|---|---|

*Sources: [Foyr](https://foyr.com), [pCon](https://pcon-planner.com), [OfficeSpace](https://www.officespacesoftware.com)*

### 3.2 AR/VR Visualization

AR and VR are transforming how clients experience office design before implementation:

**AR (Augmented Reality):**
- Smartphone/tablet-based furniture placement and scale testing
- LiDAR room scanning for precise space measurement
- Real-time material and finish swapping
- On-site client presentations with virtual furniture overlaid on physical spaces

**VR (Virtual Reality):**
- Full immersive walkthroughs of unbuilt office spaces
- 1:1 scale floor plan exploration
- Remote collaboration in shared virtual environments
- Pre-construction safety reviews

**Market context:** The AR/VR market was valued at $30.6 billion in 2022 and is projected to reach $200-521 billion by 2030-2031. Businesses using 3D configurators see a **40% increase in conversions**.

**Notable platforms:** IKEA Place (consumer AR), 3D Cloud by Marxent (enterprise, used by MillerKnoll/Herman Miller), Threekit (mid-to-large brands), Houzz (residential + commercial).

### 3.3 AI-Powered Layout Optimization

AI is the fastest-moving technology trend in this space:

**Generative Design:** AI algorithms generate multiple layout options based on constraints (headcount, space dimensions, work style preferences, building codes). What previously took architects weeks can now be accomplished in minutes.

**Key players and developments:**
- **Steelcase Casey AI:** Launched with Microsoft collaboration, achieving 72% repeat user rate, 4,700 users, and 58,000 total conversations. Trained on Steelcase research, furniture specs, and product data to speed up custom configurations.
- **qbiq.ai:** Specializes in AI-powered office space planning with generative design capabilities
- **JLL (Consulting):** Advocates AI-driven space optimization as a key differentiator in commercial real estate advisory
- **Foyr Neo:** Offers AI-rendered 4K visualizations in under 10 minutes

**Impact metrics:**
- AI integration reduces design iteration time by 60-80%
- Generative layout tools can evaluate thousands of configurations against multiple KPIs simultaneously
- Machine learning models optimize for employee satisfaction, collaboration patterns, and space efficiency simultaneously

*Sources: [qbiq.ai](https://qbiq.ai/how-to-optimize-your-office-space-planning-with-generative-ai/), [JLL](https://www.jll.com/en-us/insights/can-ai-revolutionize-office-space-design)*

### 3.4 3D Product Configurators

3D configurators are critical for office furniture e-commerce:

- Traditional photography of a sofa with 10 fabrics, 5 legs, and 3 sizes requires **150 separate photographs**; 3D configurators eliminate this entirely
- **82% of consumers** prefer immersive product views when shopping
- Leading platforms: 3D Cloud (used by MillerKnoll, HON, Herman Miller), Threekit, Mimeeq (modular office furniture specialist)
- WebAR capabilities allow AR visualization directly in the browser without app downloads

### 3.5 Digital Twins

Digital twin technology is bridging physical and digital office management:

- Building-specific digital twin market: **$4.68 billion in 2024**, growing at 27-33% CAGR
- Enables real-time monitoring of space utilization, energy consumption, and environmental conditions
- India's National Smart Grid Mission backs digital utility management for over 2,000 government buildings
- Integration with IoT sensors provides continuous data for space optimization decisions

## 4. Customer Segments

### 4.1 Large Corporates and Enterprises

**Characteristics:** Complex multi-floor, multi-location office portfolios; dedicated facility management teams; formal procurement processes; high willingness to pay for integrated solutions.

**Needs:** Enterprise-grade space planning, move management, asset tracking, occupancy analytics, integration with HRIS and building management systems, scenario planning for portfolio optimization.

**Market share:** Enterprise clients account for **54.1%** of the India flexible office market. In the IWMS market, enterprises are the largest segment.

**Key decision-makers:** Chief Real Estate Officers, VP of Facilities, Chief Operating Officers.

### 4.2 Small and Medium Businesses (SMBs)

**Characteristics:** Simpler space requirements; budget-conscious; prefer self-service tools; growing rapidly in India's tier-2 cities.

**Needs:** Easy-to-use floor planning, basic furniture layout, cost estimation, simple booking systems. Value quick time-to-value and intuitive interfaces over enterprise features.

**Market dynamics:** SMBs increasingly use coworking spaces (Indian coworking market: $271.7 million in 2024, growing at 13.2% CAGR to $829.2 million by 2033). When they do plan their own offices, they need affordable, simple planning tools.

### 4.3 Interior Designers and Architects

**Characteristics:** Professional users requiring precision, presentation-quality outputs, and manufacturer-specific product catalogs; typically work on projects for clients.

**Needs:** 2D/3D visualization, photorealistic rendering, manufacturer product libraries, BIM compatibility, export to AutoCAD/Revit, client presentation tools, collaboration features.

**Tool preferences:** Foyr Neo, SketchUp, pCon.planner, Configura CET, AutoCAD. These users drive furniture specification and selection, making them a critical channel for furniture manufacturers.

### 4.4 Facility Managers

**Characteristics:** Focused on ongoing operations rather than initial design; responsible for maintenance, space allocation changes, compliance, and cost optimization.

**Needs:** Space utilization tracking, move management, maintenance scheduling, compliance reporting, integration with building systems, desk/room booking administration.

**Market role:** Primary buyers of IWMS and CAFM (Computer-Aided Facility Management) solutions. Over 50,000 sites globally use CAFM systems.

### 4.5 Furniture Manufacturers and Dealers

**Characteristics:** Need tools that showcase their products in context; want to enable dealers and sales teams to create space plans featuring their furniture.

**Needs:** 3D product configurators, manufacturer-branded planning tools, specification and quoting integration, dealer portal functionality, product catalog management.

**Examples:** Steelcase provides SmartTools (by Configura), 2D/3D DWG symbols, and Revit models. Herman Miller/MillerKnoll uses 3D Cloud by Marxent for enterprise visualization.

## 5. Pricing Models

### 5.1 Freemium

**Model:** Free core features with paid upgrades for advanced capabilities.

**Characteristics:**
- Zero entry barrier; drives user acquisition
- Typically targets SMBs, individuals, and startups
- 72% of SaaS companies under $50M ARR offer a free tier (2025 data)
- Conversion rates typically 2-5% from free to paid

**Examples in the space:**
- Planner 5D: Free basic version, premium from $7.99/month
- SmartDraw: Free trial, then $9.95/month
- Floorplanner: Free basic, paid from $4.95/month

### 5.2 Per-Seat / Per-User Subscription

**Model:** Monthly or annual fee per user, often with tiered feature access.

**Characteristics:**
- Predictable revenue scaling with customer growth
- Standard in desk booking and space management SaaS
- Typically ranges from $3-15/user/month for basic to $50-100+/user/month for enterprise

**Examples:**
- Robin: Per-user tiered pricing
- deskbird: Per-user, Microsoft-first positioning
- Skedda: Per-bookable-space pricing ($99-199/month per tier)
- OfficeSpace: ~$60/user/year + setup fee

### 5.3 Per-Seat Professional Licensing

**Model:** Licensed per design seat, common in professional design tools.

**Characteristics:**
- Higher price points ($200-2,000+/seat/year)
- Targets professional designers and architects
- Often includes manufacturer catalogs and premium rendering

**Examples:**
- Foyr Neo: Standard plan ~$49/month (2 members), Premium ~$99/month (5 members)
- Configura CET: Enterprise pricing, per-seat licensing
- pCon.planner: Free basic version, premium features for manufacturers

### 5.4 Enterprise Licensing

**Model:** Custom contracts with negotiated pricing, typically annual.

**Characteristics:**
- Custom pricing based on portfolio size, user count, and feature requirements
- Includes implementation, training, integration, and dedicated support
- Contract values typically $50K-$500K+ annually
- Long sales cycles (3-12 months)

**Examples:**
- Archibus, IBM TRIRIGA, Planon: Enterprise IWMS contracts
- SpaceIQ, iOFFICE: Mid-market to enterprise
- 3D Cloud by Marxent: Enterprise furniture visualization

### 5.5 Emerging: Furniture-as-a-Service (FaaS)

A newer model combining furniture rental with SaaS management:
- NORNORM (Europe): Subscription-based circular office furniture
- Combines physical furniture delivery with digital space planning
- Reduces capex, supports sustainability goals
- Growing interest in India's flexible office segment

### 7.5 Market Structure Analysis

The market exhibits a **fragmented structure** with distinct layers:

1. **Enterprise IWMS Layer:** Consolidated among 4-5 major players (Eptura/Archibus/SpaceIQ/iOFFICE after consolidation, IBM TRIRIGA, Planon, Oracle, SAP). High barriers to entry due to integration complexity.

2. **Mid-Market Space Management:** More competitive with 10-15 viable players (Robin, Skedda, deskbird, OfficeSpace, etc.). Lower barriers, differentiation through UX and specific vertical focus.

3. **Design/Visualization Tools:** Fragmented with specialized players. Foyr Neo, pCon.planner, and Configura lead in furniture-specific visualization. Broad CAD tools (AutoCAD, SketchUp) serve adjacent needs.

4. **India-Specific Gap:** There is a notable gap in India-specific office planning SaaS that combines local manufacturer catalogs, India building standards, and pricing in INR. Most global tools lack deep India integration.
